Short History of System Architectures

The Monolith

We will build one great system. It will do everything. Know about everything. And we will build in in Cobol with a two digit year. What could go wrong?

Peer to Peer

We will build many systems. They will all know about each other all the time and talk to each other all the time. Together they will do everything and know about everything. And we will make sure they are interdependent. What could go wrong?

Enterprise Service Bus

We will build a transport bus to handle the communication between our systems. They wonâ€™t all have to know about each other. They will do special things and only know about what they need to know. They may operate asynchronously. Understanding the way these systems work is handled by Doug. What could go wrong?

Orchestrated Integration Architecture

We will build the transport bus. And all communication can be handled asynchronously. Systems will never know about each other. They will only send to the Orchestrator(s). The Orchestrators will maintain all the business logic of what happens between systems, speaks all of their data formats, and can maintain how all of the systems are working together at any time.
