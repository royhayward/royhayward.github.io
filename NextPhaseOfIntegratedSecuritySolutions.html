Next Phase of Integrated Security Solutions

INTEGRATION: The Process of combining different components or systems to function together as a unified whole.

In the security world, we talk about a “Physical Security Information Management” system, or PSIM. This is a Middleware Platform that is designed to pull in data from a variety of inputs and display them together. Videos and Access Control can be shown together.

Using these technologies companies and users can find out what happened during a security breach. If they catch it early enough they can even take action while the breach is ongoing. In both cases, all the information is gathered in one place.

Along with the convenience of one place to go or one pane of glass to look at for the information. Now, reports can be created from the collected data. Who is looking and doing what with the data can be more easily traced and a more pro-active process for alerting users can be created.

Reaching this stage of evolution is a major milestone in providing value to the consumers of the data. The users and companies that put these systems together save money, get more useful data and insights from it.

One of the insights that they get is that the dashboard and the systems that feed it data know about an issue before the users can respond to it. And many of the responses are not complex. These should be automated.

What Does the Future look like using ML/AI?

As organizations tried to scale the single pane of glass solution they needed more people. They needed these people to be awake, alert, attentive, and ready to respond when an event of interest happened. Scaling such a workforce was expensive and wasteful. Automation was needed to make this better.

Automation Requires:

  1. An open, easy, and non-proprietary message bus.
  1. An open, easy, and non-proprietary workflow/business logic engine
  1. Ongoing loop of evaluating and adjusting to the data stimulus that the system is providing.

Event Driven Architecture

  • Business event: Something that happened in the real business world
  • Digital event: An immutable, time-stamped digital representation of a business event
  • Event stream: An ordered flow of digital events
  • Orchestrated event: A digital event derived from contextualized event streams analysis
  • Business moment: A business event that requires "business real-time" reaction

Fast digital event capture, propagation and processing empowers real-time operations and situation awareness.

Understanding the Workflow

Events and data flow into the message bus and interact with the business logic. Commands flow back out to the security components and to the user’s dashboard.

  1. All actions that can be automated should be.
  1. Users should never be in the middle of a workflow, only at the end or the beginning.
  1. All data and actions should be saved for audits and used to improve the process for future events.

Publishing Events

Events are published to the Common Message Bus and the Orchestration engine subscribes to them and consumes them.

The Orchestration

The Orchestration engine applies its workflow business logic to the incoming messages and sends out commands to the security assets to perform the automation.

The Orchestration Engine also sends messages to the user dashboard.

Applying the ML/AI Loop

Data from Red and Green events are gathered and sent to the Machine Learning process. From time to time, the ML will produce new workflow suggestions and those get sent to update and join the business logic in the Orchestration engine.

Conclusion

At the end of this process, we have a system driven by events. Automated reactions to events and messages happen rapidly and based on multiple sources of data. Users are informed of what is happening and can also respond when needed. And all of the data from both human actions and automated actions are recorded and used to train a next level of automation.

The ultimate goal is to combine more data to allow the system to react earlier and faster to a Red Action.
