Next Phase of Orchestrated Security Solutions

Orchestration: The planning or coordination of the elements of a situation to produce a desired effect.

What Does the Future look like?

As organizations scale Single Pane of Glass solutions they need more people. They need these people to be awake, alert, attentive, and ready to respond when an event of interest happens. Scaling such a workforce is expensive and wasteful. Automation is needed to make this viable. Some automation can be achieved without changing the architecture.

For more powerful automation that lets an organization scale without prohibitive costs, an architecture change is needed.

Automation Requires:

  1. An open, easy, and non-proprietary message bus.
  2. An open, easy, and non-proprietary workflow/business logic engine
  3. Ongoing loop of evaluating and adjusting to the data stimulus that the system is providing.

Event-Driven Architecture

What are the events we are talking about in an EDA?

  • Business event: Something that happened in the real business world
  • Digital event: An immutable, time-stamped digital representation of a business event
  • Event stream: An ordered flow of digital events
  • Orchestrated event: A digital event derived from contextualized event streams analysis
  • Business moment: A business event that requires a "business real-time" reaction

Fast digital event capture, propagation and processing empower real-time operations and situation awareness.

Understanding the Workflow

Events and data flow into the message bus and interact with the business logic of the Orchestrator. Commands flow back out from the Orchestrator to the security components and to the user’s dashboard.

  1. All actions that can be automated should be.
  2. Users should never be in the middle of a workflow, only at the end or the beginning.
  3. All data and actions should be saved for audits and used to improve the process for future events.

Publishing Events

Events are published to the Common Message Bus and the Orchestration engine subscribes to them and consumes them.

The Orchestration

The Orchestration engine applies its workflow business logic to the incoming messages and sends out commands to the security assets to perform the automation.

The Orchestration Engine also sends messages to the user dashboard.

Applying the ML/AI Loop

Data from Red and Green events are gathered and sent to the Machine Learning process. From time to time, the ML will produce new workflow suggestions and those get sent to update and join the business logic in the Orchestration engine.

Conclusion

At the end of this process, we have a system driven by events. Automated reactions to events and messages happen rapidly and are based on multiple sources of data. Users are informed of what is happening and can also respond when needed. All of the data from both human actions and automated actions are recorded and used to train the next level of automation.

The ultimate goal is to combine more data to allow the system to react earlier and faster to a Red Action.
